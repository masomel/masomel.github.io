About me

I am currently a second-year M.S.E. student in the CS Department at Princeton University, and will start my Ph.D. at Princeton this coming Fall. I work with Ed Felten and Mike Freedman. My research interests are computer security, systems, networks, and applied cryptography.

I received my B.S. in Computer Science, with a Minor in Physics from Hobart and William Smith Colleges (HWS) in Geneva, NY in May 2012. I worked on two major research projects in Systems. In particular, I worked on a Computer Architecture project (LARC) and an Embedded Computing/Systems Engineering project (ELARA). I primarily worked with John Vaughn and Marc Corliss.

Personal

I consider myself a very multicultural person. My parents are from El Salvador, I'm from Miami, FL, but I grew up in a rural town near Munich, Germany. I also have an affinity for Francophone cultures, so in college, I majored in French and Francophone Studies as well. When it comes to food from other countries, I'm always open to trying new things. I recently discovered the incredibly spicy but delicious Szechuan cuisine.

In recent days, I spend most of my free time practicing and competing in Taekwondo with Princeton Taekwondo. I am also the Graduate Chair for SURGE. In my spare time, I also love to cook and bake, and enjoy a pot of tea or a tasty meal with friends. I wish I had more time to play the piano or learn a new instrument, to read, and to travel. Whenever I find time to read, I usually choose articles on science and technology, gender or environmental issues, and novels by all sorts of authors from all sorts of eras. Currently, I'm reading Bruce Schneier's Secrets and Lies.
